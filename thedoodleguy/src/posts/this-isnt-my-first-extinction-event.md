Every few years, design gets a funeral. Desktop publishing meant anyone could set type. The web meant print was dead. Squarespace meant nobody needed a designer. Canva meant it twice.

Each time, the same pattern: the floor rises, the middle hollows out, and the top gets more valuable. The work that dies is the work that was already mechanical. The work that survives is the work that was always about people.

AI is a bigger wave than the ones before it — I won't pretend otherwise. But the survival strategy hasn't changed: move up the stack. That's not theory for me; it's what I did, going from design into product management. Stop selling the artefact, start selling the outcome. Learn the new tool before others ask about it, so you're the one framing the conversation.

The designers who got hurt in past transitions weren't the least talented. They were the ones who waited for permission to change. Consider this your permission.
