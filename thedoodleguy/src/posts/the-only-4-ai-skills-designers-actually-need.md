New AI design tools launch weekly and most will be gone by Christmas. Chasing them is a treadmill. Underneath the churn, four skills keep compounding.

One: intent articulation — describing what you want with enough precision that a machine (or a human) can act on it. Two: critique at speed — when generation is free, taste becomes the bottleneck, and evaluating fifty options fast is a trainable skill. Three: workflow design — knowing where AI belongs in your process and, just as importantly, where it doesn't. Four: provenance and ethics — being able to tell a client exactly what was generated, what was made, and what rights they have.

Notice that none of these are 'learn tool X'. Tools are verbs; these are grammar. Learn the grammar and every new verb is a weekend, not a crisis.
