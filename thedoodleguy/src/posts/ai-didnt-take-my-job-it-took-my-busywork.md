I tracked one full week of work and sorted every task into two piles: things a model could do today, and things it couldn't.

The first pile was bigger than my ego wanted it to be. First-draft specs, meeting summaries, competitive teardowns, cleaning up visuals for decks, versioning assets. Hours of it. The second pile was smaller but heavier: reading a room, pushing back on a bad brief, knowing when the safe option is the wrong one.

Here's the twist — the skill that gained the most value wasn't a design skill at all. It was articulation. The better I describe intent, constraints and taste, the better every tool (and every junior, and every client meeting) performs. Prompting is just art direction with a keyboard.

The busywork pile is gone now, and I don't miss it. My week looks less like production and more like editing. That shift is uncomfortable and it is absolutely the direction of travel.
