Back in my client-facing design days, it arrived as a one-line email: "Quick q — could we just run this through Midjourney and save some budget?"

My first instinct was to defend the craft. Twenty minutes of a very passionate, very unsent reply later, I realised the client wasn't attacking my work. They were asking a fair question about value — and if I couldn't answer it, that was my problem, not theirs.

So I answered it honestly. Yes, AI could generate images cheaper. No, it couldn't decide which image was right, why it was right for their audience, or how it fit the campaign they'd spend real money running. I offered to use AI for the exploration phase — fifty directions in the time one used to take — and spend the saved hours on the part they actually pay me for: judgement.

The project got bigger. We shipped faster, explored wider, and the client felt like they were buying a process, not pixels. The lesson I keep coming back to: when a tool commoditises your output, sell your decisions instead.
